About me

PhD in molecular biology

From 2018 to 2022, I've pursued a PhD in Molecular Biology on Influenza A viruses. I worked at the IBMC (Institut de Biologie Moléculaire et Cellulaire) in Strasbourg, France, in the team "RNA packaging and viral assembly" under the supervision of Dr. Roland Marquet. My PhD aimed to obtain new insights on the packaging of Influenza A viruses through the study of RNA structural change in viral genome after viral nucleoprotein NP binding.

MSc in Bioinformatics

I witnessed along my thesis the dire need of researchers with bioinformatic skills to either analyze or develop new scripts/pipelines for viral NGS data. Thus I choose to enroll in a special master's degree at the Rouen University to gain such skills. Through my internship, I worked on the development of a new analysis pipeline dedicated to single-cell metagenomic studies for microbiota.
